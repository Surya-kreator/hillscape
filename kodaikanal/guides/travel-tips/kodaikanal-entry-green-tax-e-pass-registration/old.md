    <p class="section-tag">Introduction</p>
    <h2>Why Kodaikanal Now Requires an E-Pass and Green Tax</h2>
    <div class="divider"></div>

    <p>Kodaikanal — perched at 2,133 metres in the Palani Hills of Tamil Nadu's Dindigul district — is one of South India's most visited hill stations. Its popularity, however, came at a cost: during peak summer months, over 20,000 vehicles a day were entering a town whose fragile mountain ecology was simply not built to handle that kind of traffic. Pollution, congestion, ecological degradation, and a diminished experience for both tourists and local residents became a growing concern.</p>

    <p>Acting on petitions regarding environmental damage, the <strong>Madras High Court</strong> issued a landmark directive mandating an electronic pass (e-pass) system for all tourist vehicles entering Kodaikanal. The system went live on <strong>7 May 2024</strong>, and from <strong>1 April 2025</strong>, daily vehicle caps were formally enforced — limiting entry to <strong>4,000 vehicles on weekdays</strong> and <strong>6,000 on weekends</strong>.</p>

    <p>Separately, the <strong>Kodaikanal Municipality</strong> has long collected a <strong>green tax</strong> at the entry checkpost at Batlagundu — a conservation fee paid in cash by all visiting vehicles upon arrival. These are two distinct requirements: the <em>e-pass</em> is applied for online in advance, and the <em>green tax</em> is paid on-site at the checkpost gate.</p>

    <div class="why-box" style="margin-top:2rem;">
      <div class="why-icon">👤</div>
      <div class="why-text">
        <h4>Who This Guide Is For</h4>
        <p>This guide is for <strong>any traveller planning to drive to Kodaikanal</strong> in their own or a rented vehicle — whether arriving from Bangalore, Chennai, Madurai, Coimbatore, or any other city. It covers private cars, SUVs, motorcycles, rental vehicles, and tourist cabs. If you are travelling by state government bus (TNSTC/KSRTC), you do not need a personal e-pass. For everyone else — apply before you arrive.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="why-box">
      <div class="why-icon">⏰</div>
      <div class="why-text">
        <h4>When to Apply</h4>
        <p>Apply for your e-pass <strong>at least 2–3 days before your travel date</strong>. E-passes are issued on a first-come-first-served basis until the daily vehicle cap is reached. During peak season (April–June, long weekends, school holidays), the daily quota fills quickly — sometimes within hours of midnight on popular dates. Last-minute applications risk being denied entry. Weekend passes for April–June especially should be booked a week or more in advance.</p>

<section id="green-tax">
  <div class="container">
    <p class="section-tag">What Is It?</p>
    <h2>The Kodaikanal Green Tax</h2>
    <div class="divider"></div>

    <div class="detail-grid">
      <div class="detail-card">
        <div class="detail-label">Purpose</div>
        <p>The Kodaikanal green tax is a <strong>local entry conservation fee</strong> collected by the Kodaikanal Municipality and Dindigul District Administration at the entry checkpost. The funds are designated for the maintenance, sanitation, and ecological preservation of Kodaikanal's mountain environment — roads, drains, waste management, and green-zone upkeep that bear the burden of heavy tourist footfall. It has been collected at the Batlagundu checkpost for many years, predating the e-pass system by over a decade.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card amber-top">
        <div class="detail-label">Who Pays</div>
        <p>All tourist vehicles entering Kodaikanal must pay the green tax — regardless of whether the vehicle is registered in Tamil Nadu or another state. This includes <strong>private cars, SUVs, motorcycles, rental cars, tourist cabs, and private buses</strong>. Local residents of Kodaikanal, school buses, government vehicles, ambulances, and essential goods vehicles are typically exempt. Visitors arriving by TNSTC/KSRTC government buses do not pay separately — the tax is handled at the vehicle level.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card gold-top">
        <div class="detail-label">Applicable Vehicle Types</div>
        <p>The green tax is charged per vehicle, not per passenger:</p>
        <p>🚗 <strong>Cars &amp; Sedans</strong> — fixed per-entry fee<br>
        🚙 <strong>SUVs &amp; MUVs</strong> — slightly higher rate than cars<br>
        🏍 <strong>Motorcycles &amp; Scooters</strong> — lowest fee tier<br>
        🚌 <strong>Minibuses &amp; Tourist Vans</strong> — higher fee<br>
        🚌 <strong>Large Coaches / Private Buses</strong> — highest fee<br>
        ⚡ <strong>Electric Vehicles</strong> — may receive reduced or waived green tax; confirm at checkpost.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card earth-top">
        <div class="detail-label">How It Differs from the E-Pass</div>
        <p>These are two completely separate requirements that often confuse first-time visitors:</p>
        <p>The <strong>e-pass</strong> is your <em>permission to enter</em> — applied online in advance, free, gives you a QR code to show at the checkpost.<br><br>
        The <strong>green tax</strong> is a <em>monetary fee</em> — paid in cash at the checkpost gate when you arrive. You cannot pay it online in advance. Always carry cash for this; UPI and card are not accepted at most collection points.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<!-- ══ WHAT IS EPASS ══ -->
<section id="epass">
  <div class="container">
    <p class="section-tag">What Is It?</p>
    <h2>The Kodaikanal Vehicle E-Pass</h2>
    <div class="divider"></div>

    <div class="detail-grid">
      <div class="detail-card">
        <div class="detail-label">Purpose</div>
        <p>The TN e-pass (electronic pass) is a <strong>digital entry permit</strong> issued by the Tamil Nadu e-Governance Agency (TNEGA) through the portal <strong>epass.tnega.org</strong>. It was mandated by the Madras High Court to regulate the number of tourist vehicles entering ecologically sensitive hill stations — Kodaikanal (Dindigul district) and Ooty (Nilgiris district). The system records tourist vehicle data, enforces daily caps, and creates an auditable record of visitor inflow to support long-term environmental management.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card amber-top">
        <div class="detail-label">Who Needs It</div>
        <p>Any person travelling to Kodaikanal in a <strong>private or rented vehicle</strong> who does not reside in Kodaikanal town must obtain an e-pass before entry. This includes:<br><br>
        ✅ All out-of-state visitors (Karnataka, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, etc.)<br>
        ✅ Tamil Nadu residents outside Kodaikanal / Dindigul district<br>
        ✅ Rental car passengers and drivers<br>
        ✅ Cab and tourist vehicle operators<br>
        ✅ Motorcyclists and scooterists<br>
        ❌ Kodaikanal local residents (use <em>Localite Pass</em> option)<br>
        ❌ Government bus passengers (vehicle-level registration by operator)<br>
        ❌ Ambulances, essential services, goods vehicles</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card gold-top">
        <div class="detail-label">Validity Period</div>
        <p>The e-pass is issued for your <strong>specific travel dates</strong> as entered during registration. It is valid for the duration of stay you declare. If you overstay beyond the exit date on your e-pass, <strong>a penalty of ₹5,000</strong> applies and your vehicle registration may be blacklisted for future e-pass applications. The e-pass is non-transferable — it is linked to a specific vehicle number and mobile number. It cannot be edited after issuance; if details are wrong, apply fresh (re-application is free and instant).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="detail-card earth-top">
        <div class="detail-label">Ooty vs Kodaikanal — Are They Separate?</div>
        <p>Yes. Ooty (Nilgiris district) and Kodaikanal (Dindigul district) are different administrative areas requiring <strong>separate e-pass applications</strong> even though both use the same portal (epass.tnega.org). If you plan to visit both on the same trip, you need two separate passes — one selecting <em>Nilgiris</em> and one selecting <em>Kodaikanal</em>. Daily vehicle caps also differ: Nilgiris permits 6,000 (weekday) / 8,000 (weekend); Kodaikanal permits 4,000 (weekday) / 6,000 (weekend).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

One e-pass covers the entire vehicle; all passengers in the car are covered under the same pass. The driver's mobile number is used for OTP verification and is linked to the pass.</p>

  <h2>Two Steps, Zero Surprises at the Gate</h2>
  <p>The Kodaikanal entry requirements are straightforward once you understand the distinction: the <strong>e-pass</strong> is your free digital permission to enter — apply at <strong>epass.tnega.org</strong> at least 2–3 days before your trip. The <strong>green tax</strong> is a small cash conservation fee paid at the Batlagundu checkpost when you arrive. Both exist to protect the fragile mountain ecosystem that makes Kodaikanal worth visiting in the first place.</p>
  <p>Apply early, carry cash for the green tax, download your pass offline, and double-check your vehicle number. After that, all that remains is the ghat road, the mist rolling in at Silver Cascade Falls, and a hot cup of chocolate at Kodaikanal Lake.</p>
